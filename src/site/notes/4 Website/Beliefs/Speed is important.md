It's important to do things quickly because you can receive feedback on your actions faster and adjust your approach accordingly. You learn faster that way because learning always occurs through feedback. In turn, you get to where you want to be faster.

Speed is especially important when the world is changing around you. If you're too slow, your outputs will be obsolete by the time you're finished with them.

Examples of this in practice:
- Sending out an early draft of your writing (or testing the waters with a tweet/blog post before committing to a writing project)
- [ ] Relatedly, having an "[MVP](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Minimum_viable_product)" ready as soon as possible
- The idea that [quantity leads to quality](https://austinkleon.com/2020/12/10/quantity-leads-to-quality-the-origin-of-a-parable/?utm_source=chatgpt.com), because you get lots of 
- Decision makers are more agile (and hence more effective) when they run a fast [OODA loop](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/OODA_loop) (observe, orient, decide, act).

Counterexamples of this in practice:
- [The Beer Game](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Beer_distribution_game) (where slow feedback loops and incomplete).
